Mental Health Resources

The 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline provides free, confidential support 24/7 for people in distress.

  • Call or text: 988
  • Chat: 988lifeline.org
  • Available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week
  • Staffed by trained crisis counselors
  • Also available for those worried about someone else

This technique helps bring your attention to the present moment when you feel anxious or overwhelmed. Work through each sense slowly:

  1. 5 things you can SEE – Look around and name 5 things you can see right now.
  2. 4 things you can TOUCH – Notice 4 things you can physically feel (your feet on the floor, the texture of your clothes).
  3. 3 things you can HEAR – Listen for 3 distinct sounds around you.
  4. 2 things you can SMELL – Notice 2 scents, or recall 2 smells you enjoy.
  5. 1 thing you can TASTE – Notice any taste in your mouth, or think of a food you enjoy.

Repeat as needed. This works best when done slowly and deliberately.

The 4-7-8 breathing technique activates your body's relaxation response and can help reduce anxiety quickly. Follow these steps:

  1. Sit comfortably and close your eyes.
  2. Inhale through your nose for 4 seconds.
  3. Hold your breath for 7 seconds.
  4. Exhale slowly through your mouth for 8 seconds.
  5. Repeat this cycle 3–4 times.

Tip: Place one hand on your belly to ensure you're breathing deeply from the diaphragm, not shallowly from the chest.

Talking to someone you trust can make a significant difference. Here are some ways to reach out:

  • Friends & family: Send a simple text — "I've been struggling lately, can we talk?" is enough.
  • Therapist or counselor: Look for licensed therapists via Psychology Today or your insurance provider.
  • School counselor: If you're a student, your institution likely offers free counseling services.
  • Crisis Text Line: Text HOME to 741741 to connect with a crisis counselor via text.
  • Online therapy: Services like BetterHelp or Talkspace offer flexible, remote options.

Asking for help is a sign of strength, not weakness.
